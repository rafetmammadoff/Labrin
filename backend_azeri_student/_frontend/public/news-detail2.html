In connection with the closure of the British and US consulates in St. Petersburg, we received many questions from parents whose children are studying or planning to study in these countries. We answer the main ones.

Will my child receive a British or American visa?

As for the UK, the documents have been submitted through the visa center for the past 10 years. Closing of the consulate in St. Petersburg does not affect this process. American student visas are also issued in full-time order, but residents of St. Petersburg and Leningrad region need to come to Moscow for this. If you are planning a short-term summer program in the US, you should choose a language center that will send out an invitation for a student visa (for example, FLS or Converse).

Who will come to the rescue of my child in the event of an unforeseen situation, if the parents can not get a tourist visa?

We are fully responsible for our students until release. Each child throughout his stay in another country has an official guardian - a person who, in case of health problems or any other emergency situation, is with the ward around the clock. In addition, in the residences of foreign schools, along with children, caregivers live who monitor the safety and well-being of students on a daily basis. We declare with all responsibility that your child will in no case be left without attention and help.

Will the attitude to our children change?

IQ Consultancy and the educational institutions with which we cooperate for many years are beyond politics. Every day we receive messages from schools, universities and language centers, which talk about one thing: the most important is the future of children and the opportunities that we give them.

From the letters from educational institutions about the situation:

"We are saddened by the current disagreement between the governments of our countries. The political consequences of recent events certainly worry us all, but we would like to assure you that this is a confrontation between politicians, not between people. In the coming months, we will continue to warmly welcome all Russian students to our educational centers. "

Matthew Hurd

Oxford International Education Group
